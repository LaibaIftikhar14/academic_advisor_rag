Resume Building for University Students

A strong resume is your ticket to internships and jobs. Here is a complete guide.

Resume sections (in order):
1. Contact information (name, email, phone, LinkedIn, GitHub)
2. Education (university, degree, GPA if above 3.0, graduation date)
3. Technical skills (programming languages, tools, frameworks)
4. Work experience (internships, part-time jobs, research)
5. Projects (personal and academic projects)
6. Activities and leadership (clubs, volunteering)

Writing bullet points:
- Start with strong action verbs (built, designed, implemented, led)
- Quantify achievements when possible (increased efficiency by 30%)
- Focus on impact, not just tasks performed
- Keep each bullet to one or two lines maximum

Common mistakes:
- Including a photo (not appropriate in most countries)
- Using decorative fonts or excessive colors
- Including irrelevant personal information (age, marital status)
- Listing references on the resume itself
- Having typos or grammatical errors (automatic rejection)
- Using generic objective statements

Tailoring your resume:
- Read the job description carefully
- Include keywords from the job posting
- Emphasize most relevant experience for each application
- Adjust technical skills section to match required technologies

Getting feedback:
- University career center offers free resume reviews
- Ask professors and industry professionals for feedback
- Have peers proofread for errors
- Use the career center's resume templates as starting point
