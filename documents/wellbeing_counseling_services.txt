University Counseling and Mental Health Services

Available services:
- Individual counseling: One-on-one sessions with licensed therapist
- Group therapy: Themed groups for anxiety, depression, relationships
- Crisis intervention: Immediate support for mental health emergencies
- Psychiatric services: Medication evaluation and management
- Workshops: Stress management, mindfulness, sleep hygiene
- Online resources: Self-help tools and psychoeducation

How to access counseling:
1. Call or visit the counseling center to schedule an appointment
2. Complete initial intake paperwork online
3. Initial consultation within 1-2 weeks typically
4. Ongoing sessions scheduled based on need and availability

Crisis support:
- Crisis walk-in hours: Monday-Friday 8 AM - 5 PM
- After-hours crisis line: Available 24/7 (number on university website)
- Campus emergency services: For immediate safety concerns
- National crisis line: 988 (call or text in USA)

Confidentiality:
- All counseling services are confidential
- Exceptions: Imminent danger to self or others, abuse of minors
- Counselors are mandated reporters for certain situations
- Records are separate from academic records

Common reasons students seek counseling:
- Academic stress and performance anxiety
- Depression and persistent sadness
- Anxiety and panic attacks
- Relationship difficulties
- Grief and loss
- Adjustment to university life
- Identity exploration
- Trauma processing

Cost:
- All services free for registered students
- No insurance required
- Specialist referrals may incur costs depending on insurance
