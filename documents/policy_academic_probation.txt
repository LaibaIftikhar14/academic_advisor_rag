Academic Probation and Academic Standing Policy

Academic standing categories:

Good Standing: GPA 2.0 or above
Academic Warning: GPA 1.7 - 1.99 (one semester)
Academic Probation: GPA below 1.7 or two consecutive semesters below 2.0
Academic Suspension: Failure to improve GPA while on probation

Consequences of academic probation:
- Must meet with academic advisor every 2 weeks
- Required to develop academic improvement plan
- May be limited in course load (maximum 12 credits)
- May lose scholarship eligibility
- Priority registration suspended

Returning from suspension:
- Must sit out one full semester
- Submit readmission application
- Provide evidence of readiness to succeed academically
- Meet with dean of students before readmission

Appealing academic decisions:
- Submit appeal in writing to academic appeals committee
- Include documentation supporting your circumstances
- Appeals must be filed within 30 days of decision
- Committee response within 3 weeks of submission

Prevention and support:
- Early alert system notifies advisors when students miss classes
- Free tutoring available at academic support center
- Peer mentoring program for struggling students
- Weekly check-ins with assigned academic advisor
