Sleep Health for Academic Performance

Why sleep matters for students:
Sleep is when the brain consolidates memories and learning. Studying without adequate sleep is significantly less effective than studying with proper rest.

Sleep requirements:
- Adults (18-25): 7-9 hours per night
- Consistent sleep schedule is as important as duration
- Sleeping too long (more than 10 hours regularly) also impairs function
- Naps: 20-minute power naps can restore alertness without causing grogginess

Effects of sleep deprivation:
- 24 hours without sleep: Cognitive impairment equal to 0.10 blood alcohol level
- Chronic sleep deprivation: Memory impairment, reduced creativity, poor decision making
- Increased risk of anxiety and depression
- Weakened immune system
- Increased risk of accidents

Sleep hygiene practices:
- Consistent bedtime and wake time (including weekends)
- Dark, cool room (65-68F or 18-20C optimal)
- No screens 1 hour before bed (blue light disrupts melatonin)
- Avoid caffeine after 2 PM
- No alcohol close to bedtime (disrupts sleep quality)
- Avoid large meals within 2 hours of sleep

Pre-exam sleep strategy:
- Do not pull all-nighters before exams
- Sleep consolidates what you studied the day before
- Well-rested brain outperforms sleep-deprived brain every time
- If nervous, try relaxation techniques before bed
- A 20-minute nap the day of the exam can boost performance
