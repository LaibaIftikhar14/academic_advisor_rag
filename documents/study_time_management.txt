Effective Time Management for University Students

Time management is the most critical skill for academic success. Here are proven strategies:

1. Weekly Planning:
Create a weekly schedule every Sunday. Block time for classes, study sessions, meals, exercise, and social activities. Treat study blocks like appointments you cannot miss.

2. The Pomodoro Technique:
Study for 25 minutes, then take a 5-minute break. After 4 pomodoros, take a 30-minute break. This maintains focus and prevents burnout.

3. Priority Matrix:
Categorize tasks as urgent/important, not urgent/important, urgent/not important, not urgent/not important. Focus on important tasks before they become urgent.

4. Time Blocking:
Assign specific times for specific subjects. Do not multitask between subjects. Give each subject dedicated focused time.

5. The 2-Minute Rule:
If a task takes less than 2 minutes, do it immediately. Do not add small tasks to your to-do list.

Avoid common time wasters:
- Excessive social media browsing
- Watching videos between study sessions
- Studying with phone notifications on
- Attending unnecessary social events during exam periods
