Stress Management for University Students

Understanding academic stress:
Stress is a normal response to challenging situations. Some stress improves performance (eustress), but chronic stress harms health and academics.

Common sources of academic stress:
- Exam pressure and fear of failure
- Assignment deadlines and workload
- Financial worries and student debt
- Social pressures and relationship issues
- Career uncertainty after graduation
- Living away from family for the first time

Physical symptoms of excessive stress:
- Headaches and muscle tension
- Digestive problems
- Sleep disturbances
- Weakened immune system
- Heart palpitations
- Fatigue and low energy

Effective stress management strategies:
1. Exercise: 30 minutes of moderate exercise reduces cortisol significantly
2. Sleep: 7-9 hours is essential, not optional
3. Mindfulness: 10-minute daily meditation practice
4. Social connection: Talk to friends, family, or counselors
5. Time management: Reduce stress by staying organized
6. Perspective: Most academic setbacks are temporary

Immediate stress relief:
- 4-7-8 breathing technique: Inhale 4 counts, hold 7, exhale 8
- Progressive muscle relaxation: Tense and release muscle groups
- 5-minute walk outdoors: Fresh air and movement reset the mind
- Cold water: Splash face or briefly step outside

University stress support:
- Counseling services: Free for registered students
- Peer support groups: Connect with others experiencing similar challenges
- Wellness workshops: Check student affairs calendar
- Online resources: Headspace, Calm, BetterHelp (student discounts)
