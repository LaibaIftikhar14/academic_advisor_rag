Research Skills for University Students

Strong research skills are essential for assignments, projects, and dissertations.

Finding reliable sources:
- Peer-reviewed academic journals (highest credibility)
- Books published by academic publishers
- Government and official organization websites
- News sources for recent events
- Wikipedia for background only - never cite it directly

Database searching tips:
- Use Boolean operators: AND, OR, NOT
- Use quotation marks for exact phrases
- Use * for wildcard searches
- Filter by publication date for recent research
- Access databases through your university library

Evaluating sources (CRAAP test):
- Currency: When was it published?
- Relevance: Does it relate to your topic?
- Authority: Who wrote it and what are their credentials?
- Accuracy: Is it supported by evidence?
- Purpose: Why was it written?

Citation management:
- Use Zotero or Mendeley to organize sources
- Save full citation information immediately
- Note page numbers for direct quotes
- Follow the required citation style exactly (APA, MLA, Chicago)

Avoiding plagiarism in research:
- Always attribute ideas to their sources
- Paraphrase in your own words and cite
- Use direct quotes sparingly
- Keep notes on where each piece of information came from
