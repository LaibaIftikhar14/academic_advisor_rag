Professional Networking Guide for Students

Why networking matters:
- 70-80% of jobs are filled through networking, not job postings
- Networking provides inside information about companies and roles
- Alumni networks are powerful resources for students
- Building relationships early creates opportunities later

LinkedIn profile optimization:
- Professional headshot (significant impact on profile views)
- Compelling headline beyond just your job title
- Detailed about section with your story and goals
- All education and experience filled in completely
- Skills section with endorsements from peers
- Regular posts sharing your learning and projects

Building your network:
- Connect with all your classmates and professors
- Attend university career fairs
- Join professional organizations in your field
- Participate in hackathons and coding competitions
- Volunteer at industry conferences
- Reach out to company employees on LinkedIn

Informational interviews:
- Request 15-20 minute conversations with professionals
- Ask about their career path and day-to-day work
- Seek advice, not a job (the job often follows)
- Send a thank you note afterward
- Stay in touch periodically

Networking etiquette:
- Give before you take - share useful information first
- Follow up promptly after meeting someone
- Remember details about people and reference them later
- Do not ask for favors before establishing a relationship
- Keep commitments you make to connections
