Managing Financial Stress as a Student

Understanding student financial stress:
Financial stress is one of the leading causes of student dropout and academic difficulty. You are not alone, and there are resources available.

Common financial challenges:
- Tuition and fee costs
- Housing and food expenses
- Textbook and supply costs
- Transportation expenses
- Social activities and peer pressure spending
- Managing student loan debt

Budgeting for students:
Monthly budget template:
- Income: Part-time work + family support + financial aid
- Fixed expenses: Rent, utilities, phone
- Variable necessities: Food, transportation, personal care
- Education: Books, supplies, printing
- Savings: Even $20-50 per month helps
- Discretionary: Entertainment, dining out

Reducing expenses:
- Buy used or rent textbooks
- Cook at home rather than eating out
- Use student discounts consistently (ID card)
- Cancel unused subscriptions
- Walk or cycle instead of using rideshare
- Attend free campus events for entertainment

Increasing income:
- On-campus jobs: Library, tutoring center, dining services
- Freelancing: Use your skills for paid projects
- Research assistantships (especially graduate students)
- Selling unused items
- Participating in paid research studies

Emergency financial help:
- University emergency fund: Contact student affairs
- Food pantry: Available on most campuses
- Emergency loans: Short-term interest-free loans
- Community resources: Local food banks, assistance programs
- Do not suffer in silence - ask for help
