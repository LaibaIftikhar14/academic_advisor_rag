Academic Writing Skills Guide

Strong writing skills are essential across all university disciplines.

Essay structure:
Introduction:
- Hook: Engaging opening sentence
- Background: Context for the topic
- Thesis statement: Your main argument clearly stated

Body paragraphs:
- Topic sentence: Main point of the paragraph
- Evidence: Facts, quotes, data supporting your point
- Analysis: Explanation of how evidence supports thesis
- Transition: Link to the next paragraph

Conclusion:
- Restate thesis in different words
- Summarize main points briefly
- End with broader significance or call to action

Common writing mistakes to avoid:
- Vague thesis statements
- Unsupported claims without evidence
- Poor paragraph structure
- Inconsistent citation style
- Grammar and spelling errors
- Overly complex sentence structures

Revision process:
1. First draft: Focus on getting ideas down
2. Second draft: Check structure and argument flow
3. Third draft: Check evidence and citations
4. Final draft: Proofread for grammar and spelling

Always allow at least one day between writing and revising for fresh perspective.
