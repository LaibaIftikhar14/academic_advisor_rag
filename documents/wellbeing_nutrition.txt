Nutrition Guide for Student Academic Performance

Brain-boosting foods:
- Fatty fish (salmon, sardines): Omega-3 for brain function
- Blueberries: Antioxidants improve memory
- Nuts and seeds: Vitamin E protects brain cells
- Dark leafy greens: Vitamins K, lutein, folate
- Dark chocolate (70%+): Flavonoids improve blood flow to brain
- Eggs: Choline essential for memory
- Avocados: Healthy fats support brain function

Foods that harm academic performance:
- Processed sugary foods: Energy crashes after initial spike
- Energy drinks: Anxiety, heart palpitations, sleep disruption
- Fast food: Low nutrients, high saturated fat
- Excessive caffeine: Anxiety and disrupted sleep
- Skipping meals: Blood sugar drops impair concentration

Exam period nutrition:
- Never skip breakfast on exam day
- Eat complex carbohydrates for sustained energy (oatmeal, whole grain bread)
- Include protein to maintain alertness (eggs, Greek yogurt)
- Stay hydrated - even mild dehydration impairs cognition
- Avoid heavy meals before an exam (causes drowsiness)

Budget eating for students:
- Meal prep on Sundays for the week
- Buy seasonal fruits and vegetables
- Eggs are cheap and nutritious
- Frozen vegetables are as nutritious as fresh
- Beans and lentils are cheap protein sources
- Cook in bulk and freeze portions
