Active Recall - The Most Effective Study Technique

Active recall is the practice of actively stimulating memory during the learning process. Research shows it is significantly more effective than passive reading or highlighting.

How to practice active recall:
1. Read a section of your textbook or notes
2. Close the book
3. Try to recall everything you just read from memory
4. Check what you got right and wrong
5. Repeat for the parts you forgot

Flashcard method:
- Write questions on one side, answers on the other
- Use Anki or physical flashcards
- Review cards daily using spaced repetition
- Create cards for definitions, formulas, concepts, and processes

The Feynman Technique:
1. Choose a concept you want to learn
2. Explain it in simple terms as if teaching a child
3. Identify gaps in your explanation
4. Go back to the source material and fill those gaps
5. Simplify your explanation further

Practice testing:
- Use past exam papers
- Create your own test questions
- Answer questions under timed conditions
- Review mistakes carefully and understand why you got them wrong
