Student Entrepreneurship Resources and Guide

University entrepreneurship support:
- Entrepreneurship center offers mentorship and resources
- Startup incubator with office space for student ventures
- Pitch competitions with prize funding available
- Entrepreneur-in-residence available for consultations
- University seed fund for promising student startups

Starting a student startup:
- Validate your idea before building (talk to potential customers)
- Start with minimum viable product (MVP)
- Use lean startup methodology
- Build a diverse team with complementary skills
- Protect your intellectual property early

Legal considerations:
- Register your business (LLC or corporation)
- Student IP policy: Check if university owns your invention
- Contracts: Always have written agreements
- Data privacy compliance if handling user data

Funding sources for student startups:
- University pitch competitions
- Accelerator programs (Y Combinator, Techstars have student tracks)
- Angel investors through alumni network
- Government grants for student entrepreneurs
- Crowdfunding platforms

Balancing entrepreneurship and academics:
- Do not sacrifice your education for your startup
- Use your courses strategically (business courses for startup knowledge)
- Leverage professors as advisors and mentors
- Academic breaks are excellent times to sprint on your startup
- Set clear boundaries between startup work and study time
