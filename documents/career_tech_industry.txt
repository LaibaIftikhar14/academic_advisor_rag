Navigating the Technology Industry as a CS Student

Major technology company tiers:
- Tier 1: FAANG (Facebook/Meta, Apple, Amazon, Netflix, Google) + Microsoft
- Tier 2: Uber, Airbnb, Stripe, Snowflake, and other large tech companies
- Tier 3: Mid-size tech companies and fast-growing startups
- Tier 4: Small startups and local tech companies

Each tier offers different experiences:
- Tier 1: Highest compensation, structured programs, large scale problems
- Tier 2: Good compensation, interesting technical challenges
- Tier 3: More responsibility, equity potential, faster growth
- Tier 4: Broad experience, potentially significant equity upside

Technology roles for CS graduates:
- Software Engineer: Building products and features
- Data Scientist: Analyzing data and building models
- Machine Learning Engineer: Production ML systems
- DevOps/Cloud Engineer: Infrastructure and deployment
- Security Engineer: Protecting systems and data
- Product Manager: Defining what to build (often requires experience)

Compensation understanding:
- Base salary, bonus, and equity (stock) make up total compensation
- RSUs (Restricted Stock Units) vest over 4 years typically
- Negotiate all three components
- Use levels.fyi for compensation benchmarks

Industry certifications:
- AWS, Google Cloud, or Azure certification valuable for cloud roles
- Cybersecurity: CompTIA Security+, CEH, CISSP
- Data: Google Data Analytics, IBM Data Science
- Project Management: PMP (after gaining experience)
