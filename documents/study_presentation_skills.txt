Presentation and Public Speaking Skills for University

Many courses require oral presentations. Here is how to excel:

Preparation:
- Know your material thoroughly - do not rely on notes
- Practice out loud at least 5 times before presenting
- Time yourself to ensure you fit within the limit
- Anticipate questions and prepare answers
- Practice in front of a mirror or record yourself

Slide design principles:
- Maximum 6 words per line, 6 lines per slide
- Use large fonts (minimum 24pt)
- High contrast between text and background
- One main idea per slide
- Use images and diagrams instead of text when possible

During the presentation:
- Make eye contact with different people in the room
- Speak slowly and clearly - nerves make us speak too fast
- Use natural gestures
- Pause for emphasis on important points
- Do not read directly from slides

Handling nervousness:
- Deep breathing before presenting
- Remember: some nervousness improves performance
- Focus on the message, not yourself
- The audience wants you to succeed
- Practice is the only cure for presentation anxiety

Answering questions:
- Listen to the full question before answering
- Repeat the question for the audience
- It is okay to say "I do not know, but I will find out"
- Keep answers concise and direct
