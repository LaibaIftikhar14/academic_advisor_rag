Graduate School Application Guide

Deciding on graduate school:
- Consider your career goals - is a graduate degree required?
- Research whether industry experience first would be beneficial
- Understand the time and financial commitment
- Talk to professionals in your target field

Types of graduate programs:
- Master's degree (1-2 years): Coursework focused
- PhD (4-6 years): Research focused, often funded
- Professional degrees: MBA, JD, MD (specific professions)
- Online programs: More flexibility, may be less prestigious

Application components:
- GRE/GMAT scores (check if required - many programs dropped requirement)
- Undergraduate transcripts and GPA
- Statement of purpose (most important component)
- Letters of recommendation (3 typically)
- Resume/CV
- Writing sample (for some programs)
- Application fees ($50-$150 per school)

Statement of purpose:
- Why you want to pursue this degree
- Your research interests or career goals
- Why this specific program and university
- Your relevant experience and qualifications
- How this degree fits your long-term plans

Letters of recommendation:
- Ask professors who know your work well
- Give recommenders at least 6 weeks notice
- Provide them with your resume and statement of purpose
- Choose recommenders who can speak to your research ability

Timeline:
- Start preparing 12-18 months before target enrollment
- Take GRE/GMAT at least 6 months before deadlines
- Request letters of recommendation by October for January deadlines
- Most deadlines are December-January for fall enrollment
