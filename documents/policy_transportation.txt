Campus Transportation and Parking Policy

Parking regulations:
- All vehicles must be registered with campus security
- Annual parking permit required for regular parking
- Visitor parking available in designated lots
- Handicapped parking requires valid permit
- Fines: $50 for minor violations, $150 for fire lane parking

Campus shuttle service:
- Free for all registered students
- Routes cover all major campus areas and nearby apartments
- Schedule available on university app
- Extended service during exam periods
- Accessible vehicles available for students with disabilities

Bicycle policy:
- Bicycles must be registered with campus security
- Park only in designated bicycle racks
- Helmet required on all campus roads
- Bicycle theft is common - use quality locks
- Bicycle repair station available near main library

Rideshare and public transport:
- Uber and Lyft pickup zones designated near main entrances
- City bus stops on campus perimeter
- Monthly transit passes discounted for students
- Carpooling app available through student affairs

Walking and pedestrian safety:
- Always use crosswalks and pedestrian paths
- Campus speed limit: 15 mph
- Pedestrians have right of way at all crosswalks
- Report unsafe pedestrian conditions to facilities management
