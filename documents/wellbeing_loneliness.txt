Dealing with Loneliness and Social Isolation at University

Understanding university loneliness:
Loneliness is extremely common among university students, especially in the first year. It does not mean something is wrong with you - building a new social network takes time.

Why university can feel lonely:
- Leaving home friends and family
- Difficulty meeting people as an adult
- Social media creating false perceptions of others social lives
- Academic pressure leaving little time for socializing
- Cultural adjustment for international students
- Feeling different from peers

Practical steps to combat loneliness:
1. Join one club or organization immediately
2. Attend events even when you do not want to
3. Say yes to social invitations at least twice before declining
4. Study in public spaces (library, coffee shop) instead of alone
5. Introduce yourself to classmates regularly
6. Create small talk opportunities in dormitories

When loneliness becomes concerning:
- Persistent loneliness lasting more than a few weeks
- Avoiding all social contact
- Feeling hopeless about making friends
- Loneliness affecting sleep, appetite, or academics
- Please reach out to counseling services if this describes you

Remember:
- Nearly everyone feels lonely sometimes at university
- It gets significantly better as you settle in
- Reaching out is brave, not desperate
- Counselors are trained to help with exactly this
