Efficient Reading Techniques for Academic Texts

Academic reading is different from casual reading. These techniques will help you read more effectively.

SQ3R Method:
1. Survey: Skim the chapter headings, subheadings, and summary
2. Question: Turn each heading into a question
3. Read: Read actively to answer your questions
4. Recite: After each section, recall the key points
5. Review: Review the entire chapter after reading

Speed reading tips:
- Avoid subvocalizing (saying words in your head)
- Use a pointer (finger or pen) to guide your eyes
- Read in chunks of words rather than individual words
- Avoid rereading unless necessary

Critical reading:
- Identify the main argument
- Look for evidence supporting the argument
- Evaluate the quality of evidence
- Consider alternative perspectives
- Connect to other material you have learned

Taking notes while reading:
- Write in the margins (if your own book)
- Use sticky notes for library books
- Summarize each section in one sentence
- Mark important quotes with page numbers
- Note questions that arise as you read
