Student Code of Conduct

All students are expected to uphold the highest standards of personal and academic integrity.

Prohibited behaviors:
- Any form of academic dishonesty (see plagiarism policy)
- Harassment or discrimination of any kind
- Disruption of academic activities
- Damage to university property
- Unauthorized access to university systems
- Possession of weapons or illegal substances on campus

Reporting misconduct:
- Report to Student Affairs Office
- Report online through university reporting portal
- Anonymous reporting available
- Faculty may report directly to academic integrity office

Disciplinary process:
1. Incident report filed
2. Student notified and invited to meeting
3. Investigation conducted
4. Hearing with student conduct board
5. Decision communicated in writing
6. Appeal process available

Sanctions:
- Verbal warning
- Written warning
- Probation
- Suspension (temporary)
- Expulsion (permanent)
- Legal referral for criminal matters

Student rights during conduct process:
- Right to be informed of charges
- Right to present your account of events
- Right to have a support person present
- Right to appeal decisions
- Right to confidentiality within legal limits
