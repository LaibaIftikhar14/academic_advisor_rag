Mental Health and Academic Performance

Mental health directly impacts academic performance. Taking care of your mind is as important as studying.

Signs of academic burnout:
- Constant exhaustion despite adequate sleep
- Difficulty concentrating
- Loss of motivation and interest
- Feeling overwhelmed by small tasks
- Increased irritability and mood swings
- Physical symptoms: headaches, stomach problems

Preventing burnout:
- Schedule regular breaks and leisure activities
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule
- Exercise at least 30 minutes daily
- Eat nutritious meals regularly
- Maintain social connections outside academics
- Set realistic goals and expectations

Stress management techniques:
- Deep breathing exercises
- Progressive muscle relaxation
- Mindfulness meditation (apps: Headspace, Calm)
- Journaling thoughts and feelings
- Talking to friends, family, or counselors

When to seek help:
If you experience persistent sadness, anxiety, or thoughts of self-harm, please reach out immediately to:
- University Counseling Services
- Student Support Office
- A trusted faculty member
- National mental health hotlines

Remember: Asking for help is a sign of strength, not weakness.
