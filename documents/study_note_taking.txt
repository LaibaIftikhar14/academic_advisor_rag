Effective Note-Taking Strategies

Good notes are essential for exam preparation. Here are the best note-taking methods:

1. Cornell Method:
Divide your page into three sections:
- Right column (70%): Main notes during lecture
- Left column (30%): Key questions and cues after lecture
- Bottom section: Summary of the page in your own words

2. Mind Mapping:
- Write the main topic in the center
- Branch out to main subtopics
- Add details as smaller branches
- Use colors and symbols
- Great for visual learners and complex topics

3. Outline Method:
- Main topics as headers
- Subtopics indented below
- Details indented further
- Works well for structured subjects

4. Digital vs Paper Notes:
Research suggests handwritten notes lead to better understanding. However, digital notes are more searchable and easier to organize. Choose what works best for you.

After class:
- Review notes within 24 hours
- Fill in gaps while memory is fresh
- Highlight key concepts
- Connect new material to previous knowledge
