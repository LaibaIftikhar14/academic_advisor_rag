Freelancing Guide for CS Students

Freelancing can provide income, experience, and portfolio pieces while studying.

Getting started:
- Build a portfolio with 3-5 sample projects first
- Create profiles on Upwork, Fiverr, and Freelancer.com
- Start with lower rates to build reviews
- Specialize in 1-2 skills rather than offering everything
- Write a compelling profile that highlights your skills

Finding clients:
- Respond quickly to job postings
- Write customized proposals (not copy-paste)
- Start with smaller projects to build reputation
- Ask satisfied clients for reviews and referrals
- Network locally - many businesses need tech help

Pricing your services:
- Research market rates for your skills
- Start at $15-25/hour as a beginner
- Increase rates every 3-6 months as you gain experience
- Charge by project for well-defined work
- Always use written contracts

Managing client relationships:
- Set clear expectations upfront
- Communicate progress regularly
- Deliver slightly more than promised
- Address problems immediately and professionally
- Follow up after project completion

Legal and financial considerations:
- Keep records of all income for tax purposes
- Save 25-30% of income for taxes
- Use a separate bank account for freelance income
- Create contracts for every client engagement
- Understand intellectual property rights in client agreements
