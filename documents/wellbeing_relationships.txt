Healthy Relationships and Social Life at University

Types of relationships at university:
- Friendships: Building your social support network
- Romantic relationships: Navigating dating while studying
- Family relationships: Maintaining connections while away
- Professional relationships: Faculty, advisors, mentors

Building friendships at university:
- Attend orientation events and freshman activities
- Join clubs and organizations aligned with interests
- Engage in class discussions and group projects
- Live in residence halls for built-in social opportunities
- Be open to friendships with diverse people

Signs of healthy friendships:
- Mutual support during difficult times
- Respect for each other's boundaries
- Honesty without cruelty
- Encouragement of each other's goals
- Balance - neither person dominates the relationship

Romantic relationships at university:
- Ensure academics remain the priority
- Communicate openly about expectations
- Respect each other's study time and boundaries
- Seek help if relationship becomes controlling or harmful
- Campus domestic violence resources available

When relationships cause stress:
- Talk to a trusted friend or counselor
- University counseling offers relationship counseling
- Student affairs has resources for relationship difficulties
- Never tolerate harassment or abuse - report immediately
