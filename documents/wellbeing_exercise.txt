Exercise and Physical Activity for Students

Benefits of regular exercise for students:
- Improved concentration and focus
- Better memory and learning capacity
- Reduced anxiety and depression
- Better sleep quality
- Increased energy levels throughout the day
- Stress relief

Recommended exercise:
- 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity per week
- Or 75 minutes of vigorous activity per week
- Plus strength training 2 days per week
- This equals 30 minutes of moderate exercise 5 days a week

Exercise options available on campus:
- University gym and fitness center (free with student ID)
- Intramural sports leagues
- Group fitness classes (yoga, spinning, HIIT)
- Swimming pool and aquatic classes
- Running and walking trails on campus
- Recreation center courts (basketball, tennis, racquetball)

Fitting exercise into a busy schedule:
- Walk or cycle to class instead of driving
- Take stairs instead of elevators
- Exercise between classes (even 15 minutes helps)
- Join an intramural team for social exercise
- Work out with a friend for accountability
- Morning exercise before the day gets busy

The study-exercise balance:
- Exercise improves study sessions, not competes with them
- 30 minutes of exercise before studying improves focus
- Exercise breaks during long study sessions restore energy
- Active lifestyle improves academic performance overall
